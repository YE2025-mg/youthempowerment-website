Nomena Randriamanga Henintsoa

Born in Antananarivo, Madagascar in 2000, Nomena Randriamanga Henintsoa is a multidisciplinary professional with a strong academic background in law and economics. She holds a category B driver’s license, a DALF C2 Diploma in French, and an Advanced 1 Certificate in English. She also possesses basic knowledge of German.

Nomena consistently ranked among the top students at “École Sacrée Cœur Antanimena (ESCA)”, graduating as valedictorian. She actively participated in extracurriculars like swimming, volleyball, Vintsy environmental club, and regional volleyball competitions. She also holds a brown belt in Karate-Do.

She pursued dual academic paths: a Master's in Internal and International Public Law at the University of Antananarivo, and one in Environmental Economics and Policy at CNTEMAD. She currently holds a Master’s Diploma in Public Law with honors and is completing her second Master’s degree.

Professionally, she has interned at SAMIFIN (Madagascar’s Financial Intelligence Unit), HAISOA Madagascar as Legal and Financial Advisor, and the Ministry of Economy and Finance’s Public Finance Reform Service. Her academic research spans anti-money laundering and digital public finance reform.

As a project leader in Sustainable Development at Youth Empowerment Madagascar, Nomena works with “Team Care” on a mental health initiative involving awareness campaigns, support hotlines, and free consultations. She also engaged in the Citizenship Education Club at her university.

Nomena is known for her intellectual curiosity, adaptability, and dedication to service. With a strong academic record and professional experience, she is eager to make a positive impact in the community and the world at large.
