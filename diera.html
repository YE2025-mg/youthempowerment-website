Rakotomalala Diera Olinivo

Rakotomalala Diera Olinivo is a passionate law student specializing in Applied Private Law at the Institute of Judicial Studies, University of Antananarivo. Guided by the motto “Be the voice for the voiceless,” she is deeply committed to justice, equity, and human rights.

As an advocate for youth leadership, Diera is actively involved in Youth Empowerment Madagascar, where she works to empower young people and promote civic engagement. She is also a mentee in a prestigious leadership program for young women in Madagascar, where she refines her advocacy, public speaking, and project management skills.

Her commitment to social impact extends to the field of education. As a volunteer in an association supporting access to education for underprivileged children in public primary schools, she designs and leads activities that inspire and motivate young learners. She also organizes inter-school competitions between high schools, fostering academic excellence and healthy competition among youth.

Professionally, Diera has completed enriching internships in courts, law firms, and corporate legal departments, giving her a well-rounded understanding of both the judicial system and the legal challenges within the business world.

Driven, action-oriented, and deeply human-centered, Diera is building a future where justice, education, and equality are not distant ideals, but lived realities for all.
